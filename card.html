Gold fish

The goldfish (Carassius auratus) is a freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae of order Cypriniformes. It was one of the earliest fish to be domesticated, and is one of the most commonly kept aquarium fish.

  • Scientific Name: Carassius auratus
  • Average Length: 12 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 10 years
  • Habitat: Tropical coral reefs

A relatively small member of the carp family (which also includes the koi carp and the crucian carp), the goldfish is a domesticated version of a less-colourful carp (Carassius auratus) native to east Asia.
